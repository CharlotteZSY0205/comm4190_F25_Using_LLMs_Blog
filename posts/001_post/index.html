AI Meets Magic: A Swiftie’s Guide to Filming in Disney

In this post, I share how I used ChatGPT to design a detailed shooting script for a Taylor Swift–inspired mini music video at Disney World. From outfits and props to scene locations and camera angles, the process shows how AI can turn a creative idea into a step-by-step plan.

Prompts

I’m a Taylor Swift fan, and one of her songs, Florida, inspired me to create a small music video. Coincidentally, I’ll be visiting Orlando’s Disney World in Florida this October. I’ll be staying there for three days, and I plan to visit Hollywood Studios, Magic Kingdom, and Animal Kingdom.

During this trip, I want to film a short MV using the following excerpt from Florida. My idea is to shoot scenes inside the Disney parks with my phone, matching them as closely as possible to the imagery in the lyrics. For parts of the song that are harder to visualize literally, I’m fine with using creative interpretation and artistic freedom.

What I need from you is a very detailed script that covers every aspect of the filming process. This should include:

  • Outfit choices and styling for each scene
  • Props I should prepare
  • Specific Disney locations for each lyric line
  • Suggested camera angles and framing

Of course, since I’m a devoted Swiftie, it would be even better if the outfits, props, or chosen locations include subtle references to Taylor herself.

🎬 Detailed Script for Your Taylor-Themed Disney MV

General Concept

  • Mood: A blend of bittersweet escape, beauty, and chaos — pairing Disney’s magical spaces with the emotional rawness of the song.
  • Length: About 1–1.5 minutes (focused on the chosen lyrics fragment).
  • Style: Handheld iPhone shots, cinematic framing (vertical + horizontal mix, for TikTok/IG and YouTube).
  • Editing: Quick cuts between parks to mirror intensity; softer pans for reflective lines.
  • Color palette: Pastel pinks, midnight blues, gold shimmer (Swift-coded).

🎭 Outfits & Props

  • Day 1 (Hollywood Studios):

    • Outfit: Black skirt + silver sequin top (Reputation vibes)
    • Props: Mini microphone (nod to Taylor’s performance identity)

  • Day 2 (Magic Kingdom):

    • Outfit: White sundress + light denim jacket (Fearless/1989 vibes)
    • Props: Friendship bracelet stack (Eras Tour reference)

  • Day 3 (Animal Kingdom):

    • Outfit: Flowy bohemian dress in earthy tones (Folklore/Evermore vibes)
    • Props: Small notebook or vintage-style journal (lyricist aesthetic)

  • Optional Taylor Easter eggs: Red lipstick (Red era), cat ears headband (Olivia/Meredith nod), Polaroid camera (1989)

📍 Scene Breakdown

🎬 Scene 1 – Opening line (needing escape) 🌟🎬

  • Location: Entrance archway of Hollywood Studios
  • Shot: Wide shot, low angle, you walking through gates with sunglasses
  • Mood: Entering a “new world”
  • Editing: Fade in, handheld shaky motion

🎬 Scene 2 – Regret & burying past 🌳

  • Location: Tree of Life, Animal Kingdom
  • Shot: Medium shot, writing in journal, tearing page, leaving at roots
  • Props: Journal page = “regrets”
  • Angle: Slight tilt upward, branches filling frame

🎬 Scene 3 – “Despicable/unforgivable” intensity 👻

  • Location: Tower of Terror, Hollywood Studios
  • Shot: Close-up before ride, neon lights glowing; insert screaming ride cam shot
  • Mood: Chaotic, cathartic release

🎬 Scene 4 – “At least the dolls are beautiful” 🎠

  • Location: It’s a Small World, Magic Kingdom
  • Shot: Pan across dolls, cut to your pensive face
  • Mood: Contrast between innocence/beauty and reflection
  • Angle: Slow-motion, pastel filter

🎬 Scene 5 – “Crash / rush” 🚀

  • Location: Space Mountain, Magic Kingdom
  • Shot: Quick cuts of neon lights + motion blur
  • Mood: Adrenaline, overwhelming rush

🎬 Scene 6 – “One hell of a drug” (x2) 🌌

  • Location: Pandora – World of Avatar, Animal Kingdom (night)
  • Shot: Wide shot glowing forest → close-up spin under lights
  • Mood: Surreal, intoxicating, dreamlike

🎬 Scene 7 – “Love left me like this” (reflective moment) 🏰

  • Location: Cinderella’s Castle, Magic Kingdom (twilight)
  • Shot: Medium-long shot, sitting on ground, journal in lap, gazing upward
  • Props: Polaroid camera next to you
  • Mood: Quiet, reflective

🎬 Scene 8 – Final “Florida” outro 🎆

  • Location: Magic Kingdom fireworks
  • Shot: Wide shot from behind, your silhouette against fireworks
  • Mood: Bittersweet closure — beauty and chaos fused together

🎥 Technical Tips

Angles: Alternate between handheld close-ups (emotional intimacy) and wide establishing shots (Disney’s grandeur).

Framing: Rule of thirds, sometimes center frame for dramatic emphasis.

Camera height: Waist level for casual shots, floor-level upward for powerful shots.

Editing: Sync cuts with beat drops; overlay occasional lyric text in Taylor-style fonts.
